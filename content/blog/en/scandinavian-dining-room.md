## Scandinavian Style Dining Room – Inspiration and Arrangements

Scandinavian style is one of the most valued directions in interior design, combining simplicity, functionality, and coziness. A **Scandinavian style dining room** is the ideal place for family meals and gatherings with friends, where aesthetics go hand in hand with comfort. In this article, we'll present inspiration and tips on how to arrange such a space, as well as how to combine **a dining room with a living room in Scandinavian style**.

### Characteristic Features of Scandinavian Style

- **Light colors**: whites, grays, beiges dominate, often broken by pastel accents.
- **Natural materials**: wood, linen, cotton, wool give the interior warmth and authenticity.
- **Minimalism**: simple forms, no unnecessary decorations, focus on functionality.
- **Closeness to nature**: presence of potted plants, plant motifs in accessories.

![Scandinavian style dining room](/blog/styl-skandynawski-jadalnia-1.webp)

### Choosing Furniture for a Scandinavian Dining Room

- **Table**: the central point of the dining room. Choose a model made of natural wood with simple lines. Tables made of light wood such as pine or oak are popular.
- **Chairs**: can be wooden or upholstered in light colors. Often different chair models are used, which adds character and individuality to the interior.
- **Storage**: dressers and display cabinets made of natural wood or painted white, with simple handles or handleless.

### Scandinavian Style Dining Room Arrangements

#### Colors and Materials

- **Walls**: most often white or in light shades of gray, which optically enlarges the space.
- **Floors**: wooden boards or panels in light color. They can be complemented with a rug made of natural fibers.
- **Accessories**: textiles in subdued colors, often with geometric patterns or plant motifs.

![Scandinavian style dining room arrangement](/blog/styl-skandynawski-jadalnia-2.webp)

#### Lighting

- **Natural light**: large windows without heavy curtains, possibly light curtains or blinds that let light through.
- **Lamps**: simple, functional, often with wooden or metal elements. Pendant lamps above the table will emphasize the dining area and add coziness.

#### Accessories and Decorations

- **Plants**: green accents in the form of potted plants introduce freshness and enliven the space.
- **Art**: minimalist graphics, black-and-white photographs, posters with nature motifs or abstract patterns.
- **Dishes and table textiles**: simple ceramics, linen tablecloths and napkins that will add elegance during meals.

### Dining Room with Living Room in Scandinavian Style

In modern arrangements, you often find a **dining room combined with a living room**, creating an open and functional space. Scandinavian style works perfectly in such a layout thanks to its cohesion and universality.

#### Arrangement Tips

- **Consistent colors**: maintaining a uniform color palette in both zones will ensure a harmonious look of the entire space.
- **Space zoning**: use rugs, lighting, or furniture arrangement to separate the dining and relaxation zones.
- **Multifunctional furniture**: in smaller spaces, it's worth using furniture that can serve different functions, e.g., an extendable table or poufs with storage function.
- **Decorations**: maintain consistency in accessories, using similar materials and patterns in both zones.

### Summary

A **Scandinavian style dining room** is a combination of simplicity, functionality, and coziness. Thanks to the use of natural materials, light colors, and minimalist forms, you'll create a space ideal for shared meals and gatherings with loved ones. Remember to maintain consistency when combining the dining room with the living room, which will further emphasize the harmonious character of the interior.
